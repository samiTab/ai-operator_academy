# Team Skills & SOPs: turn your playbooks into shared muscle memory

## 1. Hook (≈15s read)
Your team's quality lives in a few people's heads and a doc nobody reads in {{industry}}. The fix isn't another wiki page — it's turning your best playbook into a **shared Skill** that everyone's Claude applies the same way, every time. Consistency at scale, without nagging — and the shared Skills you build here are the execution layer for **{{deliverable}}**.

## 2. Concept
An SOP written in a doc *describes* the right way; a **Skill** *performs* it. When you package a playbook as a shared Skill (the B3 structure — name, precise trigger, instructions, bundled templates), every teammate who triggers it gets output that meets the standard — the new hire and the veteran produce the same quality. The SOP becomes executable, not aspirational.

**Framework — ENCODE → OWN → SYNC → TEST (the shared Skill standard):**

1. **ENCODE:** Convert the SOP faithfully into a Skill — name, precise when-to-use trigger, instructions that follow the playbook's steps, and a bundled approved example. Where the SOP is ambiguous, flag it; those gaps are exactly what causes inconsistency.
2. **OWN:** Assign a named owner and a review cadence before the Skill is shared. An ownerless shared Skill drifts out of date and quietly stops being trusted. "Owner: [role] · Review: quarterly" is a one-line insurance policy.
3. **SYNC:** Keep the SOP and the Skill in sync — the SOP is the source of truth. When the playbook changes, update the Skill (and vice versa). Divergence means people get conflicting guidance from two sources.
4. **TEST:** Before rolling it to the team, have two people run it on real cases. Confirm the outputs match your standard. A shared Skill that hasn't been tested in production is a flaw-amplifier, not a quality-enforcer.

Two team-specific moves: the SOP is the source of truth, the Skill is its runtime — keep them in sync; and assign an owner, because a shared Skill without one rots.

## 4. Do it (the core — hands-on)
Pick one team playbook/SOP in {{industry}} where inconsistency costs you. Convert it to a shared Skill:

```
Convert this SOP into a shared team Skill.
SOP / PLAYBOOK: [paste your current process / standard, or describe it].
Produce a SKILL.md with:
- name + precise trigger ("use when [team situation]")
- instructions that faithfully encode the SOP's steps and standards
- a note of which template/example to bundle so output matches
Add a header line: OWNER: [role] · REVIEW: [cadence].
Flag any place the SOP is ambiguous — those are the spots that cause inconsistency.
```

Then (if you can share Skills on your team) have **two people run it on real cases**; confirm the outputs match. Note any SOP ambiguity the Skill surfaced and tighten both.

> **Privacy & safety note:** shared Skills get reused widely — keep credentials and confidential data out, use placeholders, and make sure the SOP itself is cleared for team-wide use.

### ⚠️ Common mistakes
- **No owner.** An ownerless shared Skill drifts out of date and quietly stops being trusted.
- **Skill and SOP diverge.** When one changes and the other doesn't, people get conflicting guidance.
- **Encoding an ambiguous SOP.** If the playbook is fuzzy, the Skill inherits the fuzziness — fix the SOP first.
- **Sharing without testing.** Confirm two real cases match before rolling it to everyone.

### 🔎 Honest limit
A shared Skill standardizes *how* work is done, not whether the underlying judgment is right — and it scales your SOP's flaws as efficiently as its strengths. It also doesn't supervise itself: outputs still need spot-checks, especially early. Keep an owner accountable, review on a cadence, and pair shared Skills with the escalation gates from D1 and the governance from D4.
